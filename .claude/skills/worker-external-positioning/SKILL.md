# Worker External Positioning

## Claim Rules

- The project is a harness and routing layer, not proof that cheap models are smart.
- Cost savings are credible only with metrics.
- Reliability claims require checks, receipts, trajectory evidence, or eval cases.
- Community anecdotes are leads, not proof.
- Alleged leaked prompt analyses, including the Zhihu Fable 5 breakdown, are secondary signals. Use them to discuss harness structure, skill distillation, policy placement, and tool boundaries; do not present their contents as official vendor contracts.

## Positioning Frame

- Cheap model handles bulk work.
- Harness limits damage with scope, receipts, artifacts, and checks.
- Main Codex remains the final decision layer.
- Skills preserve project doctrine for weaker sessions.
- Large monolithic prompts are a warning sign for worker design: move stable doctrine into validated skills and keep runtime prompts small, scoped, and auditable.

## When Not To Use

Do not use this for implementation details. Use `worker-architecture-contract`.

## Verification

- Cite primary sources for external claims.
- Run local metrics before quoting project performance.

## Provenance and maintenance

Revalidate market and model comparisons because they drift quickly.
